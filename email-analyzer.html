📧 Email Analysis

Most phishing attacks start with an email. Here's how to analyse a suspicious email and spot the red flags before it's too late.

🔍 Analyse This Suspicious Email

Hover over or click the highlighted parts to see what's wrong with this email.

From: support@sbi-secure-banking.net
To: you@gmail.com
Subject: ⚠️ URGENT: Your account will be suspended in 24 hours
Date: Sun, 14 Jun 2026 03:42:11 +0000

Dear Valued Customer,


We have detected suspicious activity on your account. You must verify your identity within 24 hours or your account will be permanently suspended.


Please click the button below to verify your account immediately:


🔒 Verify My Account Now

If you do not verify within 24 hours, your account access will be restricted.


Regards,
SBI Customer Support Team
support@sbi-secure-banking.net

🚩 Red Flags In This Email

  • Sender domain is sbi-secure-banking.net — not the official sbi.co.in
  • Subject line uses ALL CAPS and urgent language to create panic
  • Greeting says "Valued Customer" — real banks use your actual name
  • Creates artificial urgency — "24 hours or account suspended"
  • Link button destination is hidden — could point anywhere
  • Email sent at 3:42 AM — unusual time for official communication
  • Signature uses same fake domain as sender

✅ What To Do With Suspicious Emails

  • Do NOT click any links — go to the official website directly
  • Check the sender's full email address — not just the display name
  • Call the organisation on their official number to verify
  • Report the email as phishing in your email client
  • Delete it immediately after reporting
  • Never download attachments from suspicious emails

📋 Quick Phishing Email Checklist

Use this checklist whenever you receive a suspicious email:

☐ Does the sender domain match the official website?
☐ Does the email address you by your real name?
☐ Is the email free of urgency or threat language?
☐ Did you hover over links to verify their destination?
☐ Is the email free of spelling or grammar mistakes?
☐ Were you expecting this email?
